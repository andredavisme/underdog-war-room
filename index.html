Competitor Intelligence

How Incumbents Will Respond

Know their playbook. Counter it before they run it.

💰
Special Pricing Offers

They'll push time-limited deals close to their cost through priority channels — targeting their highest-risk accounts first. Expect bundles, rebates, and multi-year locks.

🛡️
"Trust & Reliability" Messaging

30 years in business becomes their weapon. Expect FUD: "New brands overpromise, then fail." They'll lean on case studies, SLAs, and customer tenure to create doubt.

⏱️
Delivery Upgrade Claims

Once they see our delivery advantage, they'll rush to announce improvements. Watch for announcements without real operational change. Call out the gap between promise and proof.

🔒
Account Lock-In Tactics

Long-term contracts, loyalty discounts, and referral incentives will be activated quickly. Urgency is our ally — close deals before these programs launch or extend.

📢
Brand Trust Campaigns

Expect increased advertising spend emphasizing heritage and expertise. Their message: "Don't risk it on an unknown brand." Our answer: transparent proof and visible results.

🚫
What They Won't Do

They won't fully match our price — it destroys their margin. They won't match our speed overnight — it requires real operational change. These gaps stay open if we move fast.

Sales Playbook

Prioritized Response Steps

When a prospect or existing account is being courted by an incumbent, run this sequence.

01
Validate the Real Objection

Is this about price, delivery, trust, or fear of the unknown? Identify the true concern before presenting a counter. Ask: "What would need to be true for you to move forward today?"

02
Lead with Delivery — Every Time

We reduced typical industry delivery times by over 100%. That's not a feature — that's a transformation of how their business operates. Quantify the cost of waiting: downtime, production delays, inventory carry costs.

03
Price is the Floor, Not the Ceiling

We offer lower prices — but do not sell on price alone. Present total cost of ownership: faster delivery = less downtime = real savings that dwarf the price delta. Let them do the math with you.

04
Defuse the "New Brand" Fear

Incumbents will plant doubt about our reliability. Counter with evidence: delivery records, order accuracy, support response times, and customer results. Facts kill fear. Bring data to every meeting.

05
Create Urgency Without Desperation

Incumbent special pricing is temporary and reactive. Our pricing reflects structural cost advantage. Frame urgency as: "They're discounting to slow you down. We're fast because we're built differently."

06
Close on a Pilot, Not a Promise

When hesitation is high, offer a low-risk entry: a single order or short pilot run. Proof beats persuasion every time. Once they experience the speed, the next conversation is about volume.

"They have 30 years of process. We have 30 years of their customers' frustrations."

Objection Handling

When They Push Back

Every objection is a buying signal. Here's how to respond.

"We've worked with our current supplier for over a decade."
A decade of relationship is valuable — but it also means a decade of paying more and waiting longer. I'm not asking you to end a relationship. I'm asking you to run one order alongside it and let the results decide. What's the cost of a single pilot to find out?
"You're a new brand — how do I know you'll still be around?"
Every brand that exists today was once new. What matters is operational proof: our fill rate, on-time delivery percentage, and order accuracy are trackable from day one. I'd rather you evaluate us on real data than a logo with a founding year on it. Here's what our last 90 days look like — [insert metrics].
"Our current supplier just offered us a better deal."
That deal exists because we showed up. Ask yourself: why are they offering it now, after years of standard pricing? Our price is structural — theirs is reactive. Reactive discounts end. Our cost advantage doesn't. Lock in with us now while their offer signals a market shift.
"Your delivery sounds good, but I need reliability, not just speed."
Exactly right — speed without consistency is noise. Here's our on-time fulfillment rate for the last [X] orders: [insert data]. Fast and reliable aren't in conflict for us. Our model is built around both. Would a delivery guarantee clause in the contract give you the confidence to move forward?
"I'm not sure the quality is comparable at a lower price."
That's the right question to ask, and we welcome it. Lower price doesn't mean lower quality — it means lower overhead. We don't carry legacy infrastructure costs, and we pass that savings to you. The easiest proof is a side-by-side sample. Let's run that comparison today.
